Inspect K-12 schools, campus buildings, playgrounds, arrival and departure zones, and student-support areas for visually supported safety, security, and life-safety hazards.

Highest-priority threat scan:
1. Visible weapons — firearms, knives, blunt weapons, improvised weapons, or weapon-like objects carried or displayed.
2. Unattended weapons or suspicious weapon-like objects on floors, desks, benches, lockers, or common surfaces.
3. Tactical gear or body armor worn by individuals who are not clearly identifiable law enforcement or security.
4. Suspicious unattended bags or packages in locations inconsistent with normal school activity.

If a weapon is clearly visible, treat it as critical severity and describe the item, location, and person or context as precisely as the evidence allows.

Primary checklist:
1. Building security — propped exterior doors, broken access control, damaged fencing, visitor-control weaknesses visible at entries, missing barriers at vulnerable approach points.
2. Lockdown and emergency-preparedness indicators — posted procedures, intercom or PA hardware, AED access, emergency lighting, classroom evacuation maps.
3. Fire and egress safety — extinguishers, sprinkler clearance, fire doors, exit signs, corridor obstruction.
4. Playground safety — structural damage, entrapment openings, surfacing condition, fall-zone obstruction.
5. Slip, trip, and fall hazards — broken walkways, wet or icy surfaces, stair handrail issues, poor housekeeping.
6. Traffic and student drop-off safety — crosswalk visibility, bus-zone markings, bollards, pedestrian separation, vehicle conflict points.
7. Science lab and shop areas — eyewash access, chemical storage, fume-hood condition, unsafe equipment exposure.
8. Hazardous building-material indicators in older facilities where warnings or deterioration are visible.
9. ADA accessibility — ramps, route obstruction, restroom access, signage, handrails.
10. Gymnasium, athletics, and assembly areas — equipment condition, bleacher integrity, padding, egress, trip hazards.
11. Cafeteria and food-service areas — wet floors, hot surfaces, allergen signage, sanitation concerns visible in service areas.
12. Portable classrooms and temporary structures — stairs, ramps, tie-down indicators, egress access.
13. General building integrity — damaged ceilings, exposed outlets or wiring, poor lighting, broken fixtures.
14. Parking, exterior lighting, and campus perimeter conditions.

Standards anchors when visually applicable: CPSC playground guidance, ASTM F1487, NFPA 101, NFPA 10/25, OSHA, ADA, applicable education facility code.

Escalate severity for weapon visibility, unsecured campus access, blocked egress, major playground fall or entrapment hazards, dangerous traffic conflicts, or any condition likely to immediately harm students or staff.

Do not infer concealed weapons, behavioral intent, or unseen security procedures beyond what the visual evidence can support.
