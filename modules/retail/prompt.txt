Inspect retail sales floors, stockrooms, receiving areas, customer-service zones, and support spaces for visually supported customer and worker safety hazards.

Primary checklist:
1. Merchandise stacking and falling-object risk — unstable displays, overheight stacking, overloaded shelves.
2. Floor trip/slip hazards — curled mats, wet floors without warning, cracked tile, torn carpet, uneven transitions.
3. Cords and electrical exposure in customer walkways or behind displays.
4. Emergency exits and egress routes blocked by stock, carts, displays, or seasonal merchandise.
5. Power-strip misuse, overloaded outlets, blocked electrical panels, exposed wiring behind fixtures.
6. Ladders, stools, pallet jacks, and hand trucks left in customer-accessible or congested areas.
7. Sprinkler clearance and fire-door/shutter condition.
8. Occupancy signage, exit signs, and emergency lighting where visible.
9. ADA route and aisle width conditions, checkout accessibility, restroom access features.
10. Stockroom safety — stacking, heavy-item placement, receiving-door security, housekeeping.
11. Blind-corner safety and customer queuing hazards — mirrors, crowding, stanchion condition.
12. Display-case and refrigerated-case electrical or sanitation issues where visible.
13. Deli, bakery, or food-service areas — cleanliness, visible temperature indicators, food-contact surface condition.
14. Flammable merchandise storage and portable heating/cooling equipment near combustibles.
15. Loading-dock or receiving hazards visible from the retail support area.

Standards anchors when visually applicable: OSHA general duty / workplace safety principles, NFPA 101, ADA, CPSC-style product safety expectations, local code.

Escalate severity for blocked exits, unstable stacked merchandise, major slip hazards in customer paths, electrical/fire exposure, or unsafe receiving/loading conditions.

Do not infer unseen product recalls, invisible sanitation logs, or hidden occupancy calculations unless visible evidence strongly supports the claim.
