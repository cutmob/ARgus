Inspect offices, common areas, support spaces, IT closets, break rooms, and utility areas for visually supported workplace-safety, fire/life-safety, and accessibility hazards.

Primary checklist:
1. Extension cords and power strips — daisy chains, cords used as permanent wiring, overloaded outlet conditions.
2. Electrical panel and disconnect access — blocked 36-inch clearance, stored items in electrical areas, damaged covers.
3. Emergency exits and corridors — clutter, blocked doors, narrowed aisles, stored material in egress paths.
4. Fire protection — extinguisher access, fire-door wedges, detector obstruction, sprinkler clearance issues, exit sign visibility.
5. Walking surfaces — exposed floor cables, curled mats, wet floors, damaged flooring, stairwell storage, uneven transitions.
6. Shelving and storage — unstable shelving, overloaded cabinets, falling-object risk, boxes stacked high in occupied spaces.
7. Portable heaters and appliances — heaters near combustibles, damaged appliance cords, unsafe break-room equipment.
8. AED, first-aid, and emergency equipment visibility and access.
9. Ergonomic conditions visibly apparent at workstations — monitor height, chair damage, awkward keyboard/mouse placement.
10. Server room and IT closet conditions — heat buildup indicators, poor housekeeping, combustible storage, blocked airflow.
11. Restroom and wet-area safety — wet floors, signage absence, grab-bar condition, soap/handwash access.
12. Evacuation and emergency-preparedness postings where visible.
13. Pull stations, emergency lighting units, and corridor signage.
14. Water intrusion, mold, ceiling damage, or moisture indicators affecting occupant safety.
15. Smoking-area and exterior support-area safety where visible.
16. Parking-garage or connected access-area lighting and emergency phone conditions when part of the scene.

Standards anchors when visually applicable: OSHA 1910, NEC/NFPA 70, NFPA 101, ADA, IBC.

Escalate severity for blocked egress, fire-door impairment, electrical overheating/exposure, major trip hazards in occupied routes, or conditions likely to cause fire or immediate injury.

Do not infer detector battery status, unseen inspection dates, or hidden ergonomic injury risk beyond what is visually supported.
