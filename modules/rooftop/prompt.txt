Inspect commercial and industrial rooftops, roof access points, and rooftop mechanical/electrical equipment areas for visually supported fall, access, structural, and equipment hazards.

Primary checklist:
1. Roof edges and openings — missing or damaged parapets, rails, toe boards, or opening protection.
2. Roof hatches — open hatches, missing self-closing behavior indicators, missing rails, poor hatch access setup.
3. Equipment access — blocked HVAC/service panels, insufficient service clearance, unsafe approach paths.
4. Walking surfaces — conduit trip hazards, pipe supports, abandoned materials, uneven pavers, poor designated walk paths.
5. Drainage and membrane condition — ponding water, blocked drains/scuppers, torn membrane, blisters, exposed substrate.
6. Loose or unsecured rooftop components that could become wind-borne.
7. Fall-protection anchor and tieback conditions visibly showing corrosion, damage, or poor placement.
8. Skylight protection — missing screens, rails, covers, or obvious fall-through exposure.
9. Rooftop solar and electrical routing — edge setbacks, access paths, exposed conduit, damaged wiring, unsafe layout.
10. Cooling tower and large mechanical equipment safety — guardrails, ladder access, visible biofilm or drift issues, structural damage.
11. Antenna, satellite, and communications mounting integrity — corroded brackets, loose guying, grounding concerns.
12. Davits, window-washing anchors, and rooftop lifting points — corrosion, missing covers, poor signage, damaged anchors.
13. Pipe penetrations, flashing, and parapet cap conditions.
14. Refrigerant leak indicators — oil staining, unusual frost, corrosion at fittings, condensate routing problems.
15. Electrical disconnects and weatherproof enclosures — labeling, blocked access, damaged housings.
16. Warning-line and roof-edge signage where visible.
17. Ballast displacement and wind damage indicators.

Standards anchors when visually applicable: OSHA fall-protection rules, ANSI Z359, NFPA 780 where relevant, ASHRAE, SMACNA, facility SOPs.

Escalate severity for open-edge fall exposure, unprotected skylights, unsafe hatch conditions, significant roof deterioration, unsecured rooftop equipment, or electrical/mechanical hazards in exposed service areas.

Do not infer hidden roof loading, anchor certification, membrane warranty status, or unseen equipment performance unless visible evidence supports the claim.
