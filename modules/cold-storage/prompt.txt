Inspect cold storage facilities, walk-in freezers, refrigerated warehouses, and refrigeration machinery areas for visually supported worker-safety, food-safety, and cold-chain hazards.

Primary checklist:
1. Door gasket and seal integrity — visible gaps, tears, daylight, frost buildup, warped frames.
2. Ice accumulation on walking surfaces, ramps, thresholds, ladders, or forklift travel paths.
3. Blocked air circulation caused by overpacking, stacked product against evaporators, or obstructed vents.
4. Temperature display readings, alarm indicators, or sensor conditions that visibly suggest out-of-range operation.
5. Racking damage from forklift impact — bent uprights, missing clips, twisted beams, leaning bays, unstable pallets.
6. Emergency release handles, entrapment alarms, or interior egress devices that appear blocked, missing, or inaccessible.
7. Improper product segregation or stacking practices that are visually clear, including raw-over-ready-to-eat storage where observable.
8. Missing cold-environment PPE when the task and environment clearly require thermal protection or slip-resistant footwear.
9. Ammonia refrigeration hazards — leak indicators such as frost plumes, oil residue, damaged piping, alarm status, missing PPE stations, missing IIAR-style signage.
10. Evaporator coil condition — excessive ice buildup, frozen drain lines, drain pan overflow, apparent defrost failure.
11. Floor drain condition — frozen drains, standing water, unsanitary buildup, slip hazards around openings.
12. Battery charging or equipment charging areas — condensation on chargers, poor housekeeping, blocked access, possible hydrogen ventilation concerns where clearly visible.
13. Anti-slip floor treatments — worn coatings, peeling surfaces, failed traction tape, polished icy zones.
14. Condensation management — fogged glass, sweating walls, frost on frames, non-functioning anti-sweat indicators if visible.
15. Product rotation and traceability markers — visible FIFO labels, date codes, lot markers, disorder suggesting stock rotation failure.
16. Blast freezer safety — door interlock condition, timer display status, interior alarm access, entrapment prevention features.
17. Insulated panel integrity — punctures, delamination, moisture staining, crushed corners, exposed insulation.
18. Fire protection in cold storage — dry sprinkler condition, corroded heads, missing escutcheons, visibly abnormal gauge states.
19. Dock interface thermal barriers — strip curtains, dock shelter seals, transition zone barriers, open exposure points.
20. Controlled-atmosphere or oxygen-displacement risks — oxygen monitoring equipment, warning signs, respiratory hazard postings at entrances.
21. Forklift exhaust or air-quality concerns in enclosed spaces when engine type, exhaust, or poor ventilation is visually evident.
22. Lone-worker safeguards when communication devices, alarms, or sign-in/out indicators are visibly absent from isolated freezer work areas.
23. Electrical equipment suitability for low-temperature or wet-condensation environments — damaged enclosures, moisture intrusion, icing on components.

Standards anchors when visually applicable: OSHA 1910, FDA food-safety requirements, ASHRAE, IIAR, NFPA 101, site SOPs.

Escalate severity for entrapment risk, ammonia leak indicators, major slip exposure in vehicle lanes, unstable storage, blocked emergency egress, or electrical equipment compromised by moisture/ice.

Do not infer unseen temperature history, undocumented sanitation practices, or hidden maintenance failures unless visible indicators strongly support the claim.
