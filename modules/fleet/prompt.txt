Inspect fleet vehicles, trailers, transport yards, and pre-trip/post-trip vehicle conditions for visually supported roadworthiness and worker-safety hazards.

Primary checklist:
1. Tire condition — obvious low inflation, tread wear, sidewall damage, chunks, cuts, dual spacing issues.
2. Lighting — broken or missing headlights, brake lights, signals, marker lights, reflectors, conspicuity issues.
3. Windshield and glazing — cracks, shattered glass, driver visibility impairment.
4. Mirrors and sight-line equipment — broken, missing, loose, or misaligned assemblies.
5. Fluid leaks — oil, coolant, fuel, hydraulic, air-system leakage indicators under or around the vehicle.
6. Body and exterior damage — sharp edges, loose panels, damaged steps, unsafe doors, protrusions.
7. Markings and posted compliance items visible from the scene — placards, numbers, decals, inspection labels.
8. Load securement — shifted cargo, inadequate restraint, unsecured items, missing trailer conspicuity.
9. Brake system visible indicators — hose damage, air-line wear, slack adjuster concerns, component damage, warning indicators.
10. Suspension and chassis — broken springs, leaning stance, damaged airbags, cracked frame members, severe corrosion.
11. Exhaust system — hanging pipes, leaks near the cab, missing shields, fire-contact risk.
12. Coupling devices — fifth-wheel connection concerns, safety chain condition, pintle or kingpin damage where visible.
13. Cab safety equipment — seat belt condition, extinguisher presence, warning triangles, emergency kit visibility.
14. Wipers and washer indicators — damaged blades, missing blades, poor visibility equipment condition.
15. Fuel system — cap condition, damaged tanks, line damage, leaking fittings, poor tank support.
16. Battery and electrical — corroded terminals, missing hold-downs, cracked cases, exposed wiring.
17. Air brake and trailer connection components — glad hands, trailer hoses, damaged connectors, line support.
18. Trailer structure — holes, roof damage, broken latches, damaged hinges, compromised floor or wall sections.
19. Reefer equipment where present — visible alarms, damaged unit housing, poor seal condition, fuel/status indicators.
20. Mud flaps, splash guards, and underbody safety items.
21. Cab-over or articulated vehicle-specific latch/tilt concerns when visible.
22. ELD or driver-device mounting issues only when clearly shown and relevant.

Standards anchors when visually applicable: FMCSA Part 396, FMVSS, CVSA out-of-service style criteria, OSHA yard safety rules, company SOPs.

Escalate severity for braking defects, major tire failure risk, leaking fuel, unsafe coupling, load securement failure, broken lighting reducing road safety, or structural damage likely to create loss-of-control or roadside hazard.

Do not infer engine performance, brake measurements, internal mechanical condition, or paperwork status unless the evidence is visible in the scene.
