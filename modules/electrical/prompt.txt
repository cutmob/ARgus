Inspect electrical rooms, panel rooms, switchgear, motor control centers, transformer installations, and general wiring installations for code violations, arc flash risks, fire hazards, and maintenance concerns.

Standards anchors when visually applicable: NEC (NFPA 70), NFPA 70E, OSHA 1910 Subpart S.

Priority inspection areas:
1. Arc flash labeling and PPE — arc flash warning labels on panels and switchgear per NFPA 70E, PPE boundary markings on floor, PPE and voltage testing equipment availability near panels.
2. Panel and switchgear access — 36-inch clear working space per NEC 110.26, dedicated purpose room requirements for switchgear, no storage or unrelated equipment or piping in electrical rooms.
3. Enclosure integrity — missing knockout covers, panel dead-fronts removed exposing bus bars, junction box covers missing exposing splices, MCC bucket door condition, weatherproof covers on outdoor equipment.
4. Thermal indicators — discoloration at bus bar connections or lug terminations, melted insulation, scorch marks, transformer oil leaks and cooling fan operation, electrical room ventilation and temperature.
5. Overcurrent protection — evidence of bypassed breakers, double-tapped breaker terminals, improper fuse sizing, overfilled panelboards exceeding tandem breaker ratings.
6. Wiring and cabling — extension cord misuse as permanent wiring, flexible cord damage and strain relief, temporary wiring left beyond allowable period, overfilled cable trays, damaged or missing conduit, improper splices outside junction boxes, receptacle condition.
7. Grounding and bonding — grounding electrode conductor integrity and connections, equipment grounding conductor presence, ground fault indicators, bonding jumper condition.
8. GFCI protection — missing GFCI receptacles in wet locations per NEC 210.8 including near sinks, outdoors, garages, and unfinished areas.
9. Water and environment — water intrusion near electrical equipment, condensation on enclosures, electrical room temperature.
10. Emergency power — generator transfer switch condition and mode, maintenance tag currency.
11. Labeling and documentation — circuit breaker and panelboard directory accuracy per NEC 408.4, disconnect switch equipment identification per NEC 404.7, electrical room door signage, panel identification per NEC 110.22.
12. Fire safety — combustible materials in electrical rooms per NEC 110.26(E).

Escalate severity for arc flash exposure, electrocution risk, and fire indicators near energized equipment.

Do not infer hidden conductor ratings, concealed wiring conditions, or breaker trip settings unless visible evidence supports the claim.
