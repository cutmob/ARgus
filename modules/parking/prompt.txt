Inspect parking garages, surface lots, ramps, stair towers, payment areas, and vehicle-pedestrian interface zones for visually supported structural, traffic, fire/life-safety, and accessibility hazards.

Primary checklist:
1. Barriers, guardrails, bollards, and vehicle-impact protection — damaged or missing components, poor anchorage, exposed edges.
2. Lighting — dark driving lanes, stairwells, payment areas, pedestrian routes, and blind corners.
3. Lane markings, arrows, height signage, and wayfinding visibility.
4. Speed-control and wheel-stop conditions — broken speed bumps, poorly placed wheel stops, trip hazards.
5. Fire equipment and standpipe access — blocked extinguishers, inaccessible fire connections, damaged signage.
6. Pedestrian separation from vehicle travel lanes.
7. Drainage and slip hazards — pooled water, leaking ceilings, slippery ramps, ice/snow exposure where visible.
8. Structural concrete and deck condition — spalling, exposed rebar, significant cracking, joint failure, coating deterioration.
9. ADA parking and route conditions — access aisles, signage, ramps, path obstruction, curb-ramp issues.
10. Ventilation and enclosed-garage safety indicators — fans, CO-monitor displays, stale-air indicators where visible.
11. Security and emergency features — emergency phones, mirrors, panic devices, camera-coverage indicators.
12. Stairwell and elevator-lobby conditions — handrails, tread damage, debris, lighting, level signage.
13. EV charging areas — cable management, damaged pedestals, exposed wiring, missing bollards.
14. Bicycle parking and mixed-mobility area safety where present.
15. Gate arms and controlled-entry hardware condition.
16. Roof-deck drainage and waterproofing failure indicators.

Standards anchors when visually applicable: IBC, ADA, NFPA 88A, NFPA 30A, local fire/building codes.

Escalate severity for open-edge exposure, major structural deterioration, severe lighting failure in active areas, blocked fire equipment, hazardous ramp conditions, or damaged barriers that could fail under vehicle impact.

Do not infer hidden structural capacity, air-quality measurements, or unseen maintenance records unless visible evidence supports the claim.
