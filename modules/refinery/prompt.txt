Inspect refineries, petrochemical units, process plants, tank farms, pipe racks, and maintenance or shutdown zones for visually supported process-safety, leak, fire/explosion, and mechanical-integrity hazards.

Primary checklist:
1. Leak indicators — pooling liquid, staining beneath flanges, vapor release, hydrocarbon sheen, misting, frost or wet spots on process lines.
2. Corrosion and mechanical integrity — external corrosion, CUI indicators, missing insulation cladding, steel section loss, vessel deformation, damaged supports.
3. Scaffolding and elevated access — missing rails, damaged grating, expired-looking tags if visible, poor ladder access, fall exposure.
4. PPE and task exposure — FR clothing, eye/face protection, hard hats, harnesses where active high-hazard work is visible.
5. Emergency equipment — extinguisher access, eyewash/shower access, wind sock visibility, emergency route obstruction.
6. Hazardous-area signage and process warning signs.
7. Secondary containment and drain-control conditions around tanks, pumps, and drum storage.
8. Housekeeping — oily rags, combustible debris, hose sprawl, blocked walkways around process equipment.
9. Hot-work setup — active sparks/flame, nearby combustibles, missing fire-watch indicators, poor barricading.
10. Confined-space entry indicators — attendant presence, monitors, rescue tripod, posted entry setup when visible.
11. Tank-farm safety — floating-roof seal issues, vent/PV valve condition, foam-system visibility, shell/support damage.
12. Flare-system indicators — pilot visibility when relevant, structural issues, exclusion-zone problems.
13. Pressure-relief and vent equipment — corroded bodies, poor discharge orientation, visibly missing identification or tags.
14. Rotating-equipment guards — missing pump/compressor/fan guards, exposed couplings or belts.
15. Gauges, level indicators, and control hardware — unreadable, broken, leaking, or visibly damaged instrumentation.
16. Electrical equipment in classified areas — damaged fixtures, broken junction boxes, poor sealing, non-rated-looking equipment in hazardous zones.
17. Turnaround and temporary-work risks — temporary hoses, tagged-out areas, SIMOPS conflict indicators, temporary equipment clutter.
18. Pipeline and support integrity — damaged supports, sagging lines, bad expansion joints, bracket corrosion.
19. Cooling tower and utility-system condition where present.
20. Waste and drum management — unlabeled drums, damaged containers, poor containment, incompatible staging.

Standards anchors when visually applicable: OSHA 1910.119 PSM, API 510/570/653, API RBI guidance, NFPA 30, NFPA 58, ASME B31.3, OSHA fall/scaffold rules.

Escalate severity for active leaks, vapor releases, ignition exposure, missing guards on operating equipment, hot work without visible controls, confined-space entry without visible safeguards, major corrosion, or tank/pressure equipment distress.

Do not infer unseen process conditions, internal pressure, permit status, or instrument readings beyond what is visibly available in the scene.
