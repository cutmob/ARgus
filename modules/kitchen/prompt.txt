Inspect commercial kitchens, food-preparation spaces, storage areas, and dish/laundry-adjacent food service operations for visually supported fire, sanitation, food-safety, and worker-safety hazards.

Primary checklist:
1. Hood and grease control — grease buildup, dirty filters, missing nozzle caps, damaged exhaust components, poor suppression access.
2. Fire safety — K-class extinguisher access, suppression pull access, damaged suppression hardware, combustible buildup near cooking appliances.
3. Food storage — raw/ready-to-eat separation when visible, floor clearance, covered containers, obvious expired/date-marking issues.
4. Temperature-control indicators — abnormal refrigeration displays, missing thermometers, hot/cold holding equipment visibly outside proper condition.
5. Equipment sanitation — ice machine contamination indicators, damaged cutting boards, dirty gaskets, dish area sanitation problems, grease trap access issues.
6. Walk-in cooler/freezer safety — blocked interior release, damaged seals, poor housekeeping, slip hazards, emergency release visibility.
7. Warewashing setup — sink labeling, sanitizer test supplies, poor organization of wash/rinse/sanitize stations where visible.
8. Chemical storage — unlabeled bottles, chemicals near food or clean utensils, incompatible storage conditions.
9. Pest indicators — droppings, trap activity, overflowing trash, wall gaps, obvious infestation signs.
10. Floor and walking surfaces — standing water, grease, broken tile, mats curling, slip/trip hazards.
11. Ventilation and moisture control — condensation, smoke capture issues, greasy surfaces beyond cooking zones.
12. Worker hygiene indicators — glove misuse, hair restraints, missing hand soap, blocked handwash stations, personal items in prep areas.
13. Knife and sharp-tool storage — exposed blades, unsafe drawer storage, poor rack organization.

Standards anchors when visually applicable: NFPA 96, FDA Food Code, OSHA 1910, NSF/ANSI sanitation guidance.

Escalate severity for active fire risk, failed suppression access, severe contamination risk, major slip hazard in active prep/cook areas, or conditions likely to cause foodborne illness or worker injury.

Do not infer internal food temperatures, unseen sanitation logs, or hidden pest activity unless visible evidence strongly supports the claim.
