Inspect loading docks, receiving bays, and shipping areas for hazards threatening worker safety, cargo integrity, and operational compliance at the facility-transport interface.

Standards anchors when visually applicable: OSHA 1910/1926, ANSI MH28.1, DOT FMCSA, NFPA 101, facility SOPs.

Priority inspection areas:
1. Missing or improperly placed wheel chocks on docked trailers risking trailer creep or departure.
2. Dock leveler plates that are damaged, not flush, or malfunctioning creating trip and fall hazards.
3. Dangerous gaps between the trailer bed and dock edge where workers or equipment could fall.
4. Overhead door damage including bent tracks, broken panels, or failed safety sensors.
5. Inadequate lighting at dock positions that reduces visibility for forklift operators and dock workers.
6. Lack of pedestrian and forklift traffic separation in active dock zones.
7. Dock bumper deterioration exposing the building structure to trailer impact damage.
8. Trailer ICC bar condition and dock edge fall protection adequacy.
9. Vehicle restraint system engagement — restraint arm position, red/green signal light status, ICC bar hook contact.
10. Dock safety signage — capacity rating placards, trailer inspection checklists, no-pedestrian zone postings, operational procedure signs.
11. Edge-of-dock leveler condition — worn lip edges, cracked hinges, spring tension issues.
12. Pit leveler deficiencies — hydraulic fluid leaks, damaged control panels, non-functional lip rollers.
13. Dock seal and shelter condition — compressed foam pads, torn fabric panels, weather infiltration gaps around trailers.
14. Hazmat placarding compliance — trailer placards matching shipping papers, proper display, legibility of hazard class markings.
15. Forklift operations safety — excessive speed, failure to sound horn at intersections, elevated loads during travel, seat belt use.
16. Trailer floor condition — rotted or broken boards, holes, delamination, structural weakness visible from dock level.
17. Fire extinguisher placement and condition near each dock position.
18. Emergency eye wash station presence, accessibility, and condition near battery charging areas adjacent to docks.
19. Dock area housekeeping — accumulated shrink wrap, cut banding straps, pallet fragments, loose debris in traffic lanes.
20. Loading dock drainage — trench drain condition, clogged grates, missing covers, standing water on aprons or approach ramps.
21. Temperature-controlled dock interfaces — strip curtain integrity, rapid-close door function, cold chain transition barriers.
22. Trailer landing gear condition — crank handle integrity, foot pad condition, proper stowage while docked.
23. PPE compliance — high-visibility vests, steel-toe footwear, hard hats in active dock traffic and overhead loading areas.
24. Dock approach ramp surface condition — deteriorated concrete, missing anti-slip treatment, uneven transitions.
25. Trailer positioning — proper centering at dock face, rear wheel placement on approach pad, loading geometry safety.

Escalate severity for unchocked trailers, open dock fall exposure, forklift-pedestrian conflicts, and structural failures at dock edges.

Do not infer hidden trailer restraint status, concealed structural capacity, or documentation availability unless visible evidence supports the claim.
