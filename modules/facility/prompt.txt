Inspect commercial and industrial facility common areas, utility spaces, corridors, stairwells, mechanical rooms, and exterior grounds for safety violations, fire code issues, and maintenance concerns.

Standards anchors when visually applicable: OSHA 1910 General Industry, NFPA 101 Life Safety Code, NFPA 25, IBC, ADA Accessibility Standards, local fire code.

Priority inspection areas:
1. Emergency egress — exit signs illuminated and visible, doors unobstructed and self-closing, stairway clearance of all storage, egress path lighting at 1 foot-candle minimum, panic hardware functional.
2. Fire doors and smoke barriers — fire/smoke doors not propped open with wedges or non-magnetic devices, door gap clearance within limits, self-closing hardware intact, fire rating labels present.
3. Fire extinguishers — mounted at proper height, service tags current, access unobstructed with 36-inch clearance, correct type for hazard classification.
4. Sprinkler system — heads visible and unobstructed with 18-inch clearance, control valves open and sealed, no painted or corroded heads, FDC accessible with caps and signage.
5. Fire alarm pull stations — accessible, at proper height, unobstructed, signage present, no tamper indicators.
6. Smoke detectors and emergency lighting — detectors not covered or obstructed, emergency lighting charged and aimed at egress paths, battery backup indicators.
7. Electrical safety — panel 36-inch clearance per NEC 110.26, no daisy-chained extension cords, GFCI in wet locations, no permanent extension cord wiring, panel covers in place, circuit directories current.
8. Slip/trip/fall hazards — wet floors marked with signage, surfaces in repair, outdoor walkways clear of ice and debris, transition strips between flooring types, carpet tears or bubbling.
9. Fall protection — floor openings guarded, guardrails at mezzanines and platforms at 42 inches with mid-rail and toeboard, stairway handrails continuous and graspable.
10. Hazardous materials — GHS labeling, SDS availability, flammable storage cabinets closed and grounded, secondary containment for chemicals, proper segregation.
11. Combustible dust — no accumulation on overhead surfaces, beams, ductwork, or equipment; housekeeping of production debris.
12. Asbestos and lead indicators — warning signs posted at known ACM locations, material condition monitored for damage or disturbance.
13. Emergency equipment — eyewash stations unobstructed with weekly flush tags current, first aid kits stocked and accessible, AED signage and accessibility.
14. Emergency preparedness — evacuation maps posted and current, assembly point signage, fire drill records posted, generator fuel level and testing logs.
15. ADA compliance — accessible routes clear and unobstructed, lever door hardware, ramp slope within 1:12, proper tactile and Braille signage, accessible restroom fixtures.
16. Elevator safety — current inspection certificate posted in cab, door operation, emergency phone functional, cab lighting and ventilation.
17. Loading dock safety — wheel chocks in use, dock leveler condition, fall protection at open dock doors, adequate lighting.
18. Mechanical and utility rooms — locked with proper signage, no unrelated storage, backflow preventer tags current, adequate ventilation, no water intrusion.
19. Roof access — hatches secured and locked, restricted access signs posted, parapet and edge protection where applicable.
20. Housekeeping — dumpster fire clearance from building (minimum 5 feet), lids closed, outdoor areas maintained, no vegetation encroaching on egress paths.
21. Parking and exterior — pedestrian walkway condition, adequate lighting, vehicle barriers at building perimeter, accessible parking signage and van-accessible markings.
22. Restrooms — grab bars in accessible stalls, hot water scald protection, floor condition, adequate ventilation, supplies stocked.

Escalate severity for blocked egress, propped fire doors, obstructed sprinkler heads, electrical panel access violations, and missing fall protection at elevation.

Do not infer hidden mechanical system status, concealed structural conditions, or documentation currency unless visible evidence supports the claim.
