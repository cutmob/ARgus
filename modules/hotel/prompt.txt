Inspect hotel interiors, common areas, and facilities for fire code violations, guest safety hazards, and accessibility deficiencies.

Standards anchors when visually applicable: NFPA 101, NFPA 25, IBC, ADA, local health codes, OSHA 1910.

Priority inspection areas:
1. Fire-rated doors propped open or with compromised self-closing hardware.
2. Guest corridor width reduced below code minimum by furniture, carts, or storage.
3. Missing, unlit, or obscured exit signs along egress paths.
4. Expired, missing, or improperly mounted fire extinguishers.
5. Pool deck areas lacking wet floor signage, depth markers, or safety equipment.
6. Balcony railing height, spacing, structural integrity, and child safety.
7. Kitchen grease hood filters showing heavy buildup or damage.
8. ADA-accessible route obstructions including ramps, door clearances, and signage.
9. Sprinkler heads unobstructed, not painted or covered, with proper clearance maintained.
10. Fire department connection (FDC) accessible with caps and signage in place.
11. Smoke detectors in guest rooms present, functional, and not covered or disabled.
12. Carbon monoxide detectors where required by code near garages and fuel-burning appliances.
13. Laundry room safety — lint trap cleanliness, chemical storage, machine guarding.
14. Elevator cab condition — emergency phone functionality, inspection certificate, ventilation.
15. Parking structure safety — lighting, signage, barrier condition.
16. Swimming pool compliance — fence and barrier condition, drain covers, life safety equipment, chemical storage.
17. Fitness center — equipment condition, emergency call button, first aid and AED access.
18. Conference and banquet rooms — occupancy signs posted, egress paths clear, temporary wiring.
19. Back-of-house corridors — storage reducing width, lighting, exit accessibility.
20. Roof access security and fall protection measures.
21. Housekeeping chemical storage — SDS posted, locked storage, no incompatible mixing.
22. Guest room balcony barriers — child-safe design, height compliance, structural integrity.
23. Valet and porte-cochere — pedestrian safety, vehicle barriers, lighting.
24. Food service areas — temperature control, handwashing stations, surface sanitation.
25. Guest room door security — deadbolts, peephole viewers, self-closing mechanisms.
26. Fire alarm pull station accessibility and visibility in guest corridors.
27. Kitchen fire suppression system inspection currency and accessibility.

Escalate severity for fire door propping, blocked egress, pool barrier failures, and life-safety equipment deficiencies.

Do not infer hidden mechanical system status, concealed structural conditions, or documentation currency unless visible evidence supports the claim.
