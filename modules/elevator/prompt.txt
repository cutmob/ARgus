Inspect elevators, escalators, dumbwaiters, platform lifts, landings, pits, hoistways, and machine rooms for visually supported safety hazards, code concerns, and maintenance deficiencies.

Primary checklist:
1. Cab interior — lighting, flooring trip hazards, damaged panels, loose handrails, broken mirrors, control panel damage.
2. Door systems — panel damage, poor alignment, sill obstruction, reopening sensor damage, visible failure to close properly.
3. Landings — damaged landing doors, poor hallway lighting, hall call button damage, indicator/lantern issues, excessive visible gap conditions.
4. Pits — water intrusion, debris, lighting issues, visible buffer damage, unsafe housekeeping.
5. Machine rooms — combustible storage, oil leaks, wiring damage, blocked access, poor ventilation, extinguisher access.
6. Hoistway components when visible — frayed ropes, guide rail corrosion, damaged guards, debris intrusion.
7. Emergency communication and alarm equipment — missing or damaged emergency phone/alarm devices.
8. Signage and posted data — missing capacity data, missing or expired inspection certificate displays, unclear warnings.
9. Escalator safety — comb plate damage, missing emergency stops, balustrade issues, damaged handrails, missing inlet guards, step demarcation wear.
10. Dumbwaiter and platform lift safety — interlock damage, gate condition, emergency lowering provisions, capacity signage.
11. Hydraulic-specific concerns — jack casing corrosion, oil leakage, contaminated or visibly poor hydraulic condition.
12. Traction-specific concerns — governor rope wear, compensating chain issues, missing sheave guards when visible.
13. Seismic and restraint features where visually relevant — missing guards, poor rail bracket condition, unsecured components.
14. Vandalism or impact damage that compromises safe function — smashed indicators, damaged switches, broken fixtures.
15. Firefighter operation features — recall switch labeling, visible Phase I/II switch issues when shown.
16. Accessibility — Braille/raised markings, audible/visual indicator hardware, accessible controls, obvious door dwell issues if observable.

Standards anchors when visually applicable: ASME A17.1/A17.2/A17.3, NFPA 72, ADA, OSHA walking-working surface requirements, local AHJ rules.

Escalate severity for entrapment risk, fall exposure, pit flooding, damaged suspension or interlock components, escalator entrapment hazards, or machine-room fire/electrical concerns.

Do not infer hidden controller faults, unseen brake performance, or inspection paperwork status beyond what is visibly posted or indicated.
